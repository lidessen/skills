# Association seed-library candidates

**Status:** title catalog; not a text-ingestion manifest

This catalog defines the initial cultural range available to an association
run. The built-in runtime data contains titles only: no excerpts, quotations,
summaries, locators, editions, or generated descriptions. A tool-capable Agent
may search an external source during a run; otherwise it uses fallible model
memory and must not present remembered wording as an exact quotation.

The purpose is not to turn classics into a naming dictionary. A run receives a
deterministically shuffled shelf of titles and activates the ones that resonate
against local material. The result may be any intermediate object; it does not
have to be a name, quotation, Chinese phrase, or short expression.

## Initial scope boundary

- Include the classical, historical, literary, technical, and revolutionary
  sources below.
- Include Lu Xun and *Selected Works of Mao Tse-tung* as Principal-nominated
  modern exceptions.
- Do not automatically expand into general late-Qing or Republican-era personal
  collections. Hu Shi is explicitly outside the initial scope. Add another
  modern author only through a later concrete nomination.
- Do not treat a school, dynasty, genre, or famous-author quota as evidence that
  a title is useful. The catalog controls availability; run observations decide
  later allocation.

## Explicitly outside v1

- Hu Shi and general Hu Shi collections.
- A broad “modern famous writers” bucket.
- Contemporary bestsellers or copyrighted web fiction.
- Anonymous quote databases, quote cards, generated classical-style prose, or
  attributed quotations without recoverable provenance.
- A source included only because a current model probably trained on it.

## Relation to DeepSeek training data

DeepSeek has not published a title-by-title training bibliography matching this
catalog. The [DeepSeek LLM paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2401.02954) describes a
mix of Internet text, mathematics, code, books, and self-collected data, with
filtering and deduplication. The
[DeepSeek-V3 technical report](https://arxiv.org/abs/2412.19437) reports 14.8
trillion diverse, high-quality pretraining tokens but does not disclose a book
list. Therefore it is reasonable to infer that broad book coverage contributes
to DeepSeek's Chinese associations, but it is not evidence that these specific
works or editions were in its training corpus.

Our design is materially different from pretraining: the built-in catalog is
explicit, replaceable, and auditable, while source text remains outside it. A
run records the catalog version, activated title IDs, whether activation used
memory or external retrieval, and lineage into later artifacts.
