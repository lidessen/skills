committee. The default is to keep every P-ID semantically equal and to leave no
standing committee in force until a human approves a compact roster.

## Synthesis

The reports agree that a standing role must not create semantic rank, veto power,
or a universal workflow. They also agree that P06 and P14 are strong selective
lenses rather than default liaisons. The reports diverge on P05, P07–P13, P15,
and P16. That divergence is expected: most stewards can demonstrate their own
cross-context value, which is not enough to make a compact standing roster.

The review therefore adds a maturity test to the protocol. P13–P16 are newly
adopted and have not yet survived multiple independent downstream expressions
or review cycles; none is eligible for standing membership in this cycle.

**Recommended nomination:** `P01, P02, P03, P04, P05, P08`.

This is the smallest complete general-method slate found by synthesis: judgment
over rules, grounding in reality, practice-led learning, principal contradiction,
concrete analysis, and falsifiability. P06–P12 remain ordinary members that can
lead a specific task or review when their contradiction is live. P13–P16 remain
ordinary until their maturity is demonstrated. The strongest alternative is no
standing roster at all; it preserves equality but makes every review recreate
the general-method frame.
