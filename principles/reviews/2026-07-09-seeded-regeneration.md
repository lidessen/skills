## Team formation

- **Lead P12:** tests whether organized intergenerational inheritance already

- **Standing liaison P03 (self-reviewed):** separates cumulative practice from
  repetition and tests whether a new lifecycle principle is needed for the
  spiral to continue.
- **Comparator P07:** tests whether seeded regeneration is already the movement

- **Preservation P15:** makes the strongest unchanged-sequence case and guards
  against turning the candidate into standing permission for rewrites.

### P03

## Synthesis

The reports are not votes. The recurrence, cross-context relevance, useful
boundaries, and two expression probes are sufficient to preserve the proposal
as an incubating candidate. They do not yet establish irreducibility or justify
its permanent attention cost.

The confirmed insight belongs in P12 now: continuity can be carried by a
sufficient organized inheritance rather than by keeping the original carrier
active. P07 reconstructs the successor; P15 decides whether repair,
regeneration, or retirement is the minimum valid transition; P13 and P14 protect
source obligations.

The unresolved remainder is narrower than the candidate line: retire a carrier
only after a successor demonstrates that the inherited seed can regenerate the
named capability and constraints. That is currently a migration protocol, not a
proven core principle. To reopen adoption, retain two independent real
regeneration cycles in which P07/P12/P15 were active but failed to produce the
needed succession judgment without this explicit gate.
