## Scope and default

Review the proposed P22 line, “沿可闭合边界分而治之，并在合成中恢复整体约束,”
against P01–P16. The default is to retain the current Sequence: the
[research record](../research/divide-and-conquer.md#strongest-no-proposal-case)

decomposition.

## Team formation

- **Lead P04:** tests whether a principal-contradiction diagnosis already
  decides a legitimate split or whether P22 adds a structural boundary choice.
- **Standing liaison P03:** tests whether the claimed delta has survived a
  practice–reflection–later-practice cycle; P03 is a standing member.
- **Comparator P11:** separates structural recomposition from authority to
  verify, accept, and commit a combined result.
- **Preservation P15:** makes the strongest unchanged-sequence case because its
  hard-constraint and minimum-transition rule already governs when a split is
  warranted.

## Synthesis

The reports are not counted as votes. P04 identifies a plausible structural
remainder, but P03 finds no completed learning cycle, P11 confines the
candidate’s merge to structure rather than authority, and P15 finds the
candidate’s rule a concrete expression of its existing hard-constraint test.

Therefore retain P01–P16. Keep P22 as an incubating candidate, not an alternate
or an adopted line. The next admissible evidence is two independent bounded
planning or architecture cases that first apply P04/P05/P09/P11/P15, then show
that the explicit local-closure-plus-recomposition test changes a decision those
P-IDs would otherwise leave unresolved. Each case must show that combination
preserves parent whole constraints without transferring P11/P13 authority. If
the comparison fails, settle P22 as `overlap`; if it succeeds, reconvene a
selected review before asking for a human decision.
