# P11 — Interpretation

**Sequence source:** P11｜决策权与执行权分离｜组织设计

> Read P11 in `principles/SEQUENCE.md` first.

## Shared reading

The actor that performs a change should not silently grant itself the authority
to accept, settle, or expand that change when the consequence is durable or
hard to reverse. Name who proposes, who verifies, and who commits.

## Decision questions

- Could this executor benefit from accepting its own result without independent
  evidence or authority?
- What risk would justify a separate verifier or approver here?

## Boundary and common misreading

Separation is proportional, not ceremonial. A reversible, contained action can
be decided and executed by one actor when no independent acceptance is needed.

## Theoretical roots

- Organizational design and separation-of-duty; NIST’s separation-of-duty
  principle distinguishes conflicting authorities.

## Expression probes

- A worker report remains a claim until a designated verifier accepts it.
- A committee supplies evidence but cannot adopt a sequence entry; the human can.

## Revision discipline

Revise from a failed expression probe, counterexample, or corrected source root.
Clarify who decides and commits. A claim-to-fact evidence path belongs to P13.
