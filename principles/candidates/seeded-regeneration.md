## Decision consequence

If preserving an existing project, skill, workflow, or organization has become
the obstacle to further development, do not optimize the carrier for indefinite
survival. Identify a compact inheritance that can regenerate the required
capabilities and constraints, prove it in a successor, and only then retire the
superseded carrier from the active path.

The unresolved question is whether that is an independent principle or a
project-level protocol produced by P07, P12, and P15.

## Existing-sequence check

- P03 supplies cumulative practice and reflection.
- P07 separates an inherited concrete form from its necessary determinations and
  reconstructs a new concrete.
- P12 requires a sufficient, organized inheritance and already asks what can be
  compressed, regenerated, or forgotten.
- P15 chooses among local repair, controlled regeneration, and retirement by the
  smallest transition that preserves hard constraints.

The candidate's possible remainder is a lineage-level decision: continuity may
belong to a regenerable inheritance rather than to the lifespan of its current
carrier. The review did not yet prove that this remainder changes a decision
after P07, P12, and P15 have all been applied.

## Counterexample / boundary

- Age, accumulated code, or aesthetic dissatisfaction does not justify a
  restart. If a local repair is the minimum valid transition, keep the carrier.
- A seed is not a summary or template. It must name the preserved capability,
  hard constraints, source links, and a check by which a successor can
  demonstrate recovery.
- Do not retire a source that still owns fact, audit, legal, or historical
  obligations. Archival and authority boundaries remain governed by P13 and P14.
- A destructive rewrite that cannot regenerate the protected capability is the
  crushed grain in Engels's counterexample, not dialectical development.

## Expression probes

- Skill regeneration: a new skill demonstrates the retained decision gate and
  behavior before the inherited skill is retired; source evidence remains
  inspectable without preserving its prompt shape.
- Project succession: a successor reproduces named product contracts,
  invariants, and fitness checks before the old project leaves the active path.

### Trial 1 — Principle-cultivation regeneration

**Status:** complete — overlap; the human approved
[design decision 006](../../design/decisions/006-principle-lineage-cultivation.md)
on 2026-07-10.

- **Baseline without P20:** incrementally add a research artifact and
  `research`/`propose` commands to the inherited skill while keeping its current
  workflow organization.
- **P20 delta:** extract the invariant seed—one semantic source, research before
  proposal, default preservation, selected review, separate trial/adoption
  authority, stable lineage contracts, and reconciliation—then regenerate the
  agent-facing skill around the complete lineage rather than preserving its
  numbered prompt form.
- **Disconfirming observation:** the regenerated form makes no decision the
  incremental form misses, loses an existing authority or compatibility
  boundary, increases required context without improving action, or fails the
  same action/boundary probes. Record `overlap`, `boundary failure`, or `failed`
  rather than crediting P20 merely because the rewrite succeeds.
