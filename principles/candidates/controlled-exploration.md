## Decision consequence

When formal qualification requires evidence but producing that evidence would
normally require formal qualification, do not choose only between full admission
and exclusion. If the proposed difference is credible and the risk can be
contained, create a lower-authority, bounded, reversible practice lane that can
produce decision-changing evidence without granting production, default, fact,
or semantic authority.

## Existing-sequence check

- P03 requires knowledge to return to practice, but does not allocate practice
  access or decide which unqualified proposal receives it.
- P08 requires a disconfirming observation, but does not provide the resources or
  authority needed to make that observation possible.
- P11 separates execution from durable acceptance, but can be satisfied by
  denying execution; it does not itself require a lower-authority trial lane.
- P15 bounds a chosen transition, but may also reject an experiment whose need is
  not already established.

The possible remainder is therefore an institutional choice under evidence
deadlock: preserve a path by which an unproven alternative can become knowable.
The unresolved question is whether P03, P08, P11, and P15 already produce that
choice when activated together.

## Counterexample / boundary

- Novelty creates no automatic right to attention, budget, access, or trial. A
  proposal needs a credible decision delta, a real expression probe, a fixed
  boundary, and an observation capable of defeating it.
- A trial may not bypass ethical, safety, consent, production, fact-admission, or
  semantic authority. Success produces evidence, not self-adoption.
- Use an existing evaluation path when it can expose the same difference; do not
  create experimental machinery merely to express the principle.
- Fix the resource budget and exit condition in advance. Stop on overlap,
  boundary failure, or lack of observable difference.
- Do not use P21 to authorize another candidate solely to generate evidence for
  P21 itself; that would be a self-confirming trial.
