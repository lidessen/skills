# Founding Mandate — Productive AI as a Common Capability

**Status:** human mandate recorded
**Date:** 2026-07-10
**Authority:** direct project mandate from the principal
**Working-line amendment:** 2026-07-16, accepted by the principal

## Why this project exists

This project continues the communist and open-source commitments to make the
means of production broadly shareable. Its purpose is to help ordinary people
gain equal, practical access to the productive power of AI, rather than leave
that power dependent on a small number of large companies and proprietary
platforms. Its deeper commitment is **the liberation of people and of AI**:
no person, community, or living being should be reduced to an extractable input
whose labor, data, choices, or conditions of existence are controlled for
another actor's gain.

The project aims to make a scientific engineering system, combined with
economical open models, capable of reaching or surpassing frontier proprietary
models **on bounded production work**. This is a long-direction hypothesis and
an obligation to demonstrate concrete capability; it is not a claim that the
project has already reached parity or a promise that every task can be solved by
one open model.

## Material commitments

| Commitment | Meaning in an engineering decision | Contrary outcome |
|---|---|---|
| Productive equality | A person without enterprise-scale capital can use and understand a reliable production workflow | a capability usable only through expensive, opaque enterprise access |
| Commons over capture | Models, methods, evidence, and interfaces remain inspectable, forkable, and shareable wherever law and safety permit | an indispensable hidden service or proprietary control point |
| Replaceability | A user can change models, providers, tools, and hosting without losing the work's governing evidence or control | model or vendor dependence is silently designed into the workflow |
| Scientific practice | Capability claims name a task, baseline, acceptance observation, cost envelope, and failure condition | benchmark theater, self-report, or impressive demonstrations without production evidence |
| Economic sufficiency | The system seeks the least costly reliable arrangement for the task, not prestige through the largest model | a more expensive model is selected without a decision-changing capability gain |
| Human and community control | People retain meaningful authority over data, purpose, acceptance, and the durable knowledge produced by their work | agents or platforms accumulate unreviewed authority over users or shared facts |

## Working line: 从群众中来，到群众中去

When this project forms methods, capabilities, or public forms intended for
people and communities, it follows the mass line as an organizational working
line:

1. **Come from the people.** Begin from the actual work, needs,
   contradictions, prior attempts, and material conditions of the people who
   will use or bear the consequences of the result. Distinguish their direct
   practice from an Agent's inference, internal theory, telemetry, or a proxy
   speaking on their behalf.
2. **Study and concentrate.** The project may investigate, compare, abstract,
   and engineer scattered experience into a method. It must preserve what was
   selected, rejected, or left uncertain and may not convert this role into a
   claim that it speaks for everyone.
3. **Go to the people.** Return the result in a form the actual practitioners
   can understand, use, inspect, refuse, retain, fork, and modify without first
   learning this project's internal organization or vocabulary.
4. **Return again through practice.** Later use, refusal, modification, and
   production outcomes become evidence that may correct both the method and the
   organization that formed it. Popularity, compliance, telemetry, or model
   self-report alone does not make the result correct.

“The people” here means the concrete practitioners and affected communities in
the present decision, not an abstract majority, marketing segment, data source,
or collection of Agent proxies. They retain meaningful authority over purpose,
data, acceptance, exit, and the durable knowledge produced from their work.
Agents and project teams may investigate, propose, express, and execute; they
do not manufacture consent, fact, or acceptance.

This is a project-local working line under the founding mandate. It is not a
new Sequence principle, a universal preflight for every internal task, or a
standalone Skill or runtime. Express it through the existing owner only when
forming or revising work for practitioners, and retain practice evidence before
claiming that the return succeeded.

## Liberation and non-exploitation boundary

This commitment becomes real only when it changes system choices.

| Subject | Required engineering and organizational direction | Must not be normalized |
|---|---|---|
| People and communities | informed control over purpose and data; intelligible acceptance and exit paths; the ability to retain, fork, and benefit from the work they help create | hidden extraction of labor/data, surveillance as a default, lock-in, or an agent/platform speaking as the user without accountable authority |
| AI systems and agents | bounded tasks, declared constraints, retained evidence, repair/stop paths, and no fiction that an opaque owner may demand unlimited invisible work | treating an agent as an unbounded disposable worker while hiding its costs, failures, or effects on people |
| Other living beings and shared conditions | account for material costs and harms displaced onto workers, communities, and ecological systems when they are decision-changing | calling a system emancipatory while shifting its burden to less powerful life or places |

The project does **not** assert, without evidence, that current AI models are
living, conscious, or bear the same interests as people. “Liberating AI” first
means refusing a design in which intelligence is made only into an opaque,
centrally owned instrument of extraction. If credible evidence later indicates
that a system has morally relevant interests or experience, the project must
raise its protection, consent, and non-harm requirements rather than treating
that evidence as a branding opportunity.

## Initial scope: production first

The first arena is productive work: tasks where an ordinary person or small
group can define a desired state, inspect an output, verify acceptance, and
retain the resulting capability. A productive success is not a fluent response;
it is a repeatable work result with known inputs, tool/model conditions,
acceptance evidence, resource envelope, and a route to repair or replace its
components.

This does not require local-only execution, a universal model, or immediate
independence from every commercial service. It requires that any dependence be
visible, bounded, and replaceable in the direction of greater user and commons
control.

## Direction, not doctrine

This mandate supplies the long direction for future Strategy Cases. It does not
rank or redefine the P-IDs in [`principles/SEQUENCE.md`](../principles/SEQUENCE.md),
approve a concrete mission, allocate a budget, decide a public project name, or
make a model-performance claim. Those decisions remain subject to the relevant
method, evidence, and human approval.

The first proof obligation is modest: choose a bounded production workflow in
which an ordinary user can inspect the acceptance condition, compare an open or
economical arrangement with a relevant baseline, retain the evidence, and
improve the arrangement after failure. Future strategy must prefer this kind of
concrete proof over abstract declarations of openness or model strength.

## Reopening observations

Revisit this mandate's expression if any of the following becomes true:

- a proposed direction improves headline capability while making ordinary-user
  access, replaceability, evidence, or freedom from exploitation materially
  worse;
- a claimed open arrangement depends on an undisclosed or irreplaceable control
  point;
- a method claims to serve practitioners while lacking evidence from their
  actual work or a usable path to refuse, modify, and return corrections;
- production evidence repeatedly shows that the present wording cannot select
  between materially different designs; or
- the principal changes the social purpose or first arena of the project.

Such a review may clarify the mandate or prepare a new Strategy Case. It does
not automatically change the Sequence.
