## Two formations, two objects

| Formation | Object of review | Produces | Cannot do |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Founding review committee** | project content: mandate, Sequence, methods, runtime, evidence carriers, public/aesthetic expression, and their coherence | substantive opinion on whether the proposed project baseline is faithful and usable | execute patches, operate the PR process, vote facts into existence, or merge |
| **Preparation group** | project operation and maintenance: worktree/branch route, PR completeness, CI, review-thread handling, handoff, records, and recovery | operational-readiness report and remaining gates | redefine project content, substitute its opinion for the committee, or merge |

The committee is a temporary content-review formation projected from the [Principle Council](../../../principles/COUNCIL.md); it is not a permanent board or a second semantic source. The preparation group is the temporary formation of the [formal operations campaign](2026-07-10-formal-operations-preparation.md); it ends after the founding transition. This report contains isolated role-separated passes by the current session, so it remains evidence—not independent AI review evidence or a Principal merge decision.

## Committee content review

### Formation

| Seat | Content question |
|---|---|
| **P04 — lead** | Does the baseline address the project's principal contradiction—open, economical, verifiable productive AI capability—rather than merely reorganizing a skills repository? |
| **P02 — material seat** | Are the central claims, limits, and current capabilities grounded in actual artifacts and observations rather than desired identity? |
| **P07 — architecture seat** | Does the path from principles through skills and Work Cell return to concrete, usable practice? |
| **P13 — evidence seat** | Are claims, raw evidence, verification, human acceptance, and non-accepted experiments distinguished? |
| **P14 — inheritance seat** | Do archive, Chronicle, run records, and projections preserve source authority without creating competing project truth? |

### Deliberation

| Content area | Committee observation | Opinion |
|---|---|---|
| Founding mandate and identity | The mandate names common, inspectable, replaceable productive capability and explicitly rejects exploitation. The project name remains deliberately pending; `skills` is still only a repository handle. | Faithful; name deferral does not weaken the present content baseline. |
| Sequence and active methods | One citation-free Sequence remains the semantic root; interpretations, research, candidates, reviews, skills, and portable snapshots are downstream. New methods have named scope and authority boundaries rather than a universal task loop. | Coherent and materially more usable than the former mixed active/legacy surface. |
| Work Cell | The runtime provides bounded evidence, not planning or semantic authority. The three external review observations now have focused fixes and 20 passing deterministic tests; its budget-exhaustion traces are retained as limitations rather than claimed success. | Suitable as an experimental practice unit; not yet evidence of general autonomous repair capability. |
| Organization, operations, and Chronicle | Formal operations retain human initiation and merge authority. The Chronicle distinguishes source records from receipts and projections. The reflection sidecar records decision-changing anomalies without creating a self-reflection daemon. | Correctly bounded for a pilot; scheduled/event triggers and branch protection remain deferred. |
| Aesthetic practice and archive | The aesthetic material is retained as a provisional, human-accepted seed with reuse limits; retired skills and articles move to archive instead of remaining competing active surfaces. | Content is preserved without presenting a generated asset or legacy method as universal doctrine. |

### Committee opinion

The committee finds the **project content conditionally fit to become the first accepted baseline**. It recommends no split into artificial partial PRs because the mandate, README, design, workflow, runtime, and methods cross-reference one another. Its material reservation is not a content defect: the baseline is still experimental in Work Cell execution, aesthetic practice, and Chronicle operation, and the documents state those limits rather than hiding them.

## Preparation group operational-readiness review

### Evidence

The group transferred every retained source change from the founding-dirty root to one clean worktree. The [founding-tranche ledger](2026-07-10-founding-tranche-ledger.md) uses five review partitions within one PR; local residue remains excluded.

At the reviewed commit, remote `verify` passed. Local snapshot validation, Chronicle validation/tests, workflow YAML parse, `git diff --check`, Work Cell typecheck, and 20 deterministic tests passed. The group triaged the three AI review observations, implemented focused fixes, and retained their external threads for explicit disposition rather than silently treating CI as review.

### Operational opinion

The baseline is **operationally prepared but not yet ready for merge**:

1. Three external review threads need an evidence-backed reply and explicit resolve/reject action.
2. Independent AI review evidence must be retained as a compact, traceable packet; this report is not a substitute.
3. The Principal must then decide merge, return-to-work, or hold. Branch protection, project naming, F3 shadow missions, and F4 remote settings stay outside this disposition.

## Joint recommendation to the Principal

**Continue review; do not merge yet.** The committee supports the content of the founding baseline. The preparation group reports that the operational path is ready for the final review steps but has not completed them. When the three threads have explicit dispositions and independent AI review evidence is retained, the integration steward may present the ordinary merge/return decision to the Principal.

## Reopening observation

Reopen content review if a reviewer identifies a cross-partition contradiction, the fixes fail from a fresh checkout, or an artifact cannot be placed under the mandate/Sequence/runtime/record boundary. Reopen operational review if CI fails on the current head, a thread remains unaccounted for, or recovery/cleanup cannot follow the operating protocol.
