# Aesthetic Case — Common Workshop Masthead

**Status:** accepted as reference material; public placement pending
**Practice source:** [aesthetic practice pilot](../README.md)
**Seed:** [initial preference probe](../seed/2026-07-10-initial-preference-probe.md)

## Concrete object and audience

Create an unbranded, reusable masthead study for a README, documentation page,
or future project introduction. A new reader should perceive concrete shared
production and open technical practice before reading the detailed method list.

## Direction

- **Subject:** a human-scale public workshop with repairable tools, notebooks,
  modular computing material, and no heroic or proprietary figure.
- **Composition:** a clear editorial grid with Constructive Commons geometry;
  bold structure must support orientation, not imitate a political poster.
- **Material:** warm paper, charcoal, muted vermilion, indigo depth, and small
  living material cues; the result should be calm enough for documentation.

## Constraints

- No words, logo, flags, brand marks, model-provider references, or human faces.
- No claim that the image is a project identity, a final public asset, or proof
  of openness/capability.
- Preserve a wide central/left region that could later coexist with real title
  and introductory copy, but do not generate placeholder text.

## Acceptance and review

The study is useful if the Principal can recognize shared productive practice,
structural clarity, and living material atmosphere without seeing it as a
corporate AI hero image, a nostalgic workshop photograph, or propaganda.

If accepted for a specific surface, a later asset record must name its exact
placement, license/reuse decision, accessibility alt text, and relation to the
chosen project identity.

## Principal observation

The Principal approved the generated masthead as aesthetic material. It is
therefore retained as a reference for the named direction, but this approval
does not place it in the README or make it a final public project asset.
