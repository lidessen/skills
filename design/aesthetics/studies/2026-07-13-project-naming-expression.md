# Aesthetic Study — Project Naming Expression

**Status:** observed failure; no direction promoted
**Practice source:** [Aesthetic Practice Pilot](../README.md)
**Technical evidence:** [activation-field scale probe](../../../regeneration/evaluations/2026-07-13-activation-field-scale-probe.md#human-rejection-of-the-archive-surface)

## Object, audience, and action

The object is a possible long-term name for the whole project. The immediate
audience is the Principal, who must be able to recognize whether a candidate has
enough natural life to merit further development. A later public audience must
be able to say and remember the name before learning its full history.

## Observed failure

The Principal rejected the first blind comparison before choosing a side: all
eight surfaces felt unfamiliar and mannered. The rejected packet had sampled
only novelty-oriented archive niches, so the comparison offered two variants of
the same distortion and omitted an absolute `reject both` action.

Two later, self-evaluated probes did not return to the Principal as candidate
work:

1. A plain-expression instruction converted the distortion into generic English
   craft compounds rather than natural names.
2. Supplying contrastive aesthetic evidence inside the generating prompt caused
   surface imitation: it revived `Gongqi / 公器`, which the Principal had already
   rejected, and produced arbitrary foreign words or constructed compounds.

An independent, surface-only filter then allowed three reads to reject every
candidate. Mouth-and-ear fit, cultural naturalness, and capacity to accrue
meaning each rejected all twelve names. This was same-model filter evidence, not
human aesthetic acceptance, but it successfully withheld a known-bad set from
human review.

## Learning retained

- Divergent associative material is private working material, not a public
  expression or candidate merely because it is novel.
- Naturalness cannot be implemented as a ban on unfamiliar forms; that turns
  generation toward generic labels.
- Preference examples are observations, not prompt templates. A generator that
  copies their language, length, cultural register, or mechanism has not learned
  the preference relation.
- Aesthetic review needs an absolute rejection path before relative comparison.
  A separate gate may protect human bandwidth, but it cannot accept a name.

## Boundary

This study does not establish a naming style, approve a project name, promote a
shared aesthetic direction, or prove that a same-model gate has taste. The
[initial visual seed](../seed/2026-07-10-initial-preference-probe.md) remains
visual preference evidence and must not be generalized into a naming rule.

## Next disconfirming practice

Test whether retrieval of a small set of human-observed positive and negative
naming cases changes formation without copying their surfaces. Compare it with a
no-case baseline, preserve the all-reject gate, and require human review before
claiming improvement. The treatment fails if it imitates approved examples,
repeats rejected formation patterns, or sends another wholly rejected set to the
Principal.
