# 001 — Selective Principle Committee

**Status:** adopted

## Context

The sequence needs adversarial review before it grows, but an all-principle
committee would turn every review into ceremony and encourage a second canon of
stored interpretations.

## Decision

The sequence is the central committee: every P-ID retains equal semantic
authority. `principle-cultivation` will form a temporary working team for each
sequence addition, revision, or retirement. A human-approved standing committee
keeps the general line; its members remain P-IDs, not stored interpretations.
Each team selects a lead, a standing liaison, 1–3 direct comparators, and a
preservation seat that makes the strongest case for retaining the sequence.
The coordinator records why each 3–5 seats were selected; it never defaults to
the whole sequence. P01, P10, and P15 are common preservation perspectives,
but none is a permanent veto.

The team produces independent evidence, overlap analysis, boundaries, and an
unchanged-sequence alternative. It does not vote, interpret the sequence
persistently, or modify it. Human approval remains the only adoption authority.

## Consequences

- New principles face explicit cost, redundancy, and preservation tests.
- Skills become durable teams: one leading principle plus a small working group.
- Review scope remains proportional to the decision rather than sequence size.
- Review records preserve reasoning without competing with the sequence.
- A direct human adoption is retained and receives a retrospective review rather
  than being silently reopened or reversed.
