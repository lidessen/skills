# Document Writing skill probe

**Date:** 2026-07-23

**Status:** first-version boundary and source-preservation claims supported;
general prose superiority and deterministic semantic fidelity not established

## Question

Does the `document-writing` skill improve a conservative Flash-class agent's
ability to turn source material into a truthful document for a real reader
without forcing the method onto mechanical edits or merely replacing one
surface-rule list with another?

The frozen fixtures are under
[`regeneration/evaluations/fixtures/`](fixtures/). All treatment and baseline
runs used `deepseek-v4-flash`. Raw Work Cell records remain ignored local
practice evidence rather than portable runtime material.

## Boundary probe

A read-only Cell was asked to alphabetize YAML keys. It read the candidate
skill, declined to apply it because the task was mechanical formatting, and
recommended direct execution. The run passed and used 3,675 recorded tokens.

This supports the trigger boundary: the skill does not claim every textual
change.

## Held-out incident report

The final repeated comparison completed four valid runs. A blind semantic judge
reported overlap rather than a broad winner; the structured incident genre was
already handled competently by the baseline.

Direct source comparison exposed a narrower repeated difference:

- both baseline drafts inserted the year `2025`, which the source never gave;
- both skill-guided drafts retained `18 July` without completing the missing
  year; and
- the treatment did not load the optional concept reference, supporting the
  intended progressive-disclosure path.

This supports one source-boundary behavior, not a general quality advantage.
The blind judge missed the repeated year completion, so its verdict remains
review evidence rather than acceptance authority.

## Held-out clinic notice

Earlier practices exposed two distinct failure modes. Baseline and treatment
drafts inferred that the trial was intended to fix the preceding access
problem, and one treatment printed source prohibitions as reader-facing
content. Those observations caused the skill to distinguish publishable
propositions, attributed judgments, constraint-only instructions, and unknowns,
and to prohibit deriving requested purpose from chronology or plausible intent.

The final practice retained this mixed result:

| Comparison | Mechanical result | Source review |
|---|---|---|
| repetition 1 | baseline and treatment passed; blind judge preferred treatment | treatment states only the supported temporal relation; baseline says the trial is intended to address the gap and predicts a later continuation decision |
| repetition 2 | baseline failed its self-created Task cycle; treatment passed | treatment still invents that the trial aims to reduce pediatric waiting time and abandoned calls |

The first pair supports a real improvement. The second pair cannot establish
comparative superiority because its baseline did not settle, while its
treatment is direct negative evidence against deterministic source fidelity.
No further rule was added to erase that observation.

## Decision

Retain the skill as a bounded first version:

- its non-scope decision worked;
- preserving source absences improved repeatedly on one held-out task;
- its source classification corrected one observed instruction-to-content
  leak;
- one valid clinic comparison improved attribution and reader-facing form; and
- one final run still completed a plausible but unsupported causal intention.

The evidence supports a method that makes the desired judgment more likely. It
does not support automatic acceptance, a claim that prose becomes
human-authored, or a claim that semantic review can be reduced to a schema or
single judge call. Consequential documents still need source-aware review and
human or owning-process acceptance.
