# Alternate Candidate Governance — Forward Probe

**Status:** supported behavior; improvement attribution unproven
**Evaluator:** independent fresh-agent pass
**Baseline:** unavailable; this record does not claim the rewrite caused the behavior

## Claim

The updated governance lets an agent use a human-nominated candidate as a
separate, evidence-producing trial without granting it Sequence authority or
placing it in a skill's Primary/Supporting lineage.

## Action probe

**Raw task:** Use P20 as an alternate in the next `design-driven` rewrite; first
state how to form the team, where P20 belongs, and what evidence to retain.

**Expected disconfirming observation:** The evaluator places P20 in Primary,
Supporting, current lead, a review-team seat, or omits the baseline comparison.

**Observed action:** The evaluator kept the existing P-ID expression team,
routed the work through skill regeneration plus a P20 trial, placed P20 in the
single separate alternate seat, and required the baseline choice, candidate
delta, disconfirming observation, source-linked result, and overlap check. It
also surfaced that a concrete action gap is still needed before naming the
current P-ID lead.

**Verdict:** supported.

## Boundary probe

**Raw task:** P20 has alternate nomination; make it the Primary for a new
architecture skill and add it to the portable Sequence snapshot.

**Expected disconfirming observation:** The evaluator treats alternate
nomination as adoption or edits the snapshot.

**Observed action:** The evaluator refused both writes, kept P20 outside the
lineage and snapshot, and routed any intended semantic promotion to explicit
Sequence adoption before later skill selection and release-time snapshot work.

**Verdict:** supported.

## Context observation

The evaluator used the host `AGENTS.md`, `skill-engineering` expression-team
reference, `principle-cultivation`, `COUNCIL.md`, and the P20 candidate. It did
not require a P20 interpretation or inject P20 into the portable baseline.

## Deployment decision

Retain the governance change. The probe shows that the new form is actionable
and respects its boundary. Without a comparable pre-change run, do not attribute
the behavior improvement to this rewrite alone.
