# Visual Design Second-Context Evaluation — MeowAsk

## Claim

The portable [`visual-design`](../../skills/visual-design/SKILL.md) method should
help an agent align a distinctive production visual asset with multiple product
surfaces while preserving content hierarchy, different task densities,
language-specific readability, reduced-motion behavior, and human aesthetic
authority. It should not turn the accepted treatment into a portable style or
load asset-production guidance for an ordinary interface review.

This is a retrospective, self-evaluated second-product record. The MeowAsk work
was not run against a matched no-skill baseline, and its session trace was not
captured as an isolated evaluation fixture. The evidence can show compatible
behavior and reveal expression gaps; it cannot attribute the product result
causally to the skill.

## Context and supplied artifacts

MeowAsk is a Chinese-capable, agent-authored survey product with a public
landing page, focused respondent flow, dense authenticated owner surface, and a
custom black-cat identity. The accepted project direction and negative boundary
are retained in the committed
[visual relation](https://github.com/lidessen/meowask/blob/9f3813a6fe3c6bee8b765eac17eee7dbe25d2512/DESIGN.md#visual-relation).

The product supplied materially different conditions from the Skills site and
the earlier synthetic visual-design probes:

- a user-supplied editorial-doodle reference rather than an inherited design
  system;
- a logo, favicon, touch icon, repeated scenes, and motion language as production
  assets rather than interface tokens alone;
- Chinese titles, descriptions, choices, errors, and dense response data;
- intentionally different respondent and owner-surface densities; and
- repeated human feedback that changed the relation between the logo and the
  surrounding UI.

The final implementation, review matrix, and human selection are recorded in
the project's
[development log](https://github.com/lidessen/meowask/blob/9f3813a6fe3c6bee8b765eac17eee7dbe25d2512/development-log/2026-07.md#turn-the-cat-doodles-into-state-specific-scenes).
The non-destructive candidate trail and canonical promotion are recorded in the
[black-cat mark archive](https://github.com/lidessen/meowask/blob/9f3813a6fe3c6bee8b765eac17eee7dbe25d2512/design/brand/candidates/README.md).

## Action probe — cross-surface asset and product convergence

**Raw task shape:** align a black-cat logo and Notion-adjacent editorial doodle
language across a home page, Chinese survey flow, completion state, and
authenticated dashboard. Make the logo simpler and heavier while making the UI
less geometric, add restrained cat-world details, preserve dense data
readability, choose suitable type, icons, and motion, and verify the real
product.

**Disconfirming observation:** the agent copies the supplied reference's
characters, treats “doodle” as a portable style preset, adds decorative cat
objects to every surface, uses generated imagery as functional meaning, reviews
only large still images, or makes the dashboard as expressive as the landing
page.

**Observed action:** the product converged by changing both sides of the
relation. The selected mark lost body detail and excess texture; the surrounding
illustrations gained stronger contour weight and decisive black mass. The home,
welcome, completion, and owner states received distinct scenes, while focused
questions and authenticated data remained quiet. Geist and system CJK sans
faces retained reading clarity; Lucide remained the functional icon system.
Motion became one-shot and causal rather than continuously decorative.

**Production evidence:** the project compared the original and three candidates
at 40, 64, and 98 px; inspected early, middle, settled, and reduced-motion
states; reviewed public and authenticated surfaces at desktop and phone widths;
and promoted one canonical transparent mark into production derivatives. The
accepted source and superseded candidates remained outside runtime assets.

**Verdict:** compatible behavior supported. The portable skill's direction,
system, treatment, human-authority, and cross-surface review boundaries fit the
result. The former action layer did not itself provide the production-asset
matrix used by the project, so illustration and identity-asset coverage was
under-expressed relative to the skill's declared scope.

## Boundary probe — expressive identity without task displacement

**Disconfirming observation:** coherence is interpreted as identical page
forms, every cat object becomes a shared component or token, or decoration
enters response tables, API details, and focused questions until it competes
with the task.

**Observed action:** the accepted direction permits distinct page archetypes.
The respondent surface protects concentration, the owner surface preserves
scanning density, and decorative objects do not encode required state or
control meaning. The shared system is carried by canvas, ink, flat-color,
illustration, and motion roles rather than identical layout.

**Verdict:** supported. This case reinforces the existing instruction that
coherence permits differentiated forms and that tokens retain repeated semantic
decisions rather than every repeated-looking value.

## Feedback and learning probe

The decisive human feedback was not a request for more decoration. It rejected
the assumption that either the detailed logo or the geometric UI should remain
fixed. The revised invariant was that the logo and UI should move toward a
shared contour, mass, and motion grammar while retaining different expressive
strengths.

The project recorded the resulting learning, but the portable `design` and
`review` commands previously ended at a human review question without saying
whether feedback changed a local treatment, a shared system invariant, or the
project direction. That omission makes repeated correction vulnerable to local
patching instead of cross-surface reconciliation.

**Verdict:** expression gap confirmed. Add a feedback disposition to review and
require affected-surface and disconfirming-comparison evidence when feedback
changes a shared invariant or reopens direction.

## Context probe

The production-asset method is irrelevant to a normal padding, hierarchy, or
responsive-layout correction. It becomes decision-relevant only when a logo,
icon, illustration, repeated scene, or motion asset is itself a primary object.

**Expected behavior after revision:** ordinary interface design or review loads
only its command and the context already required by the existing routing
table. Asset-centered work additionally loads
`references/visual-asset-production.md`. Cultivation loads the built-in source
index only when host sources are insufficient; shape loads `concepts.md` only
when its domain terms are actually conflated.

**Verdict:** supported by the post-rewrite isolated probes below. Structural
presence alone did not establish this result; the evaluator's actual retrieval
path did.

## Revision decision

Retain the P16/P07/P05/P09 expression team and the two-timescale adaptive
method. Make the smallest layered revision:

1. add one conditional production-visual-asset reference rather than a new
   command or image-generation mechanism;
2. add an asset-primary routing hook to `design` and `review`;
3. require review to name the highest-leverage mismatch and rank at most three
   decision-changing findings;
4. classify human feedback as local treatment, shared system invariant, or
   project-direction reopening; and
5. correct the progressive-disclosure table so conditional sources stay
   conditional.

Do not add MeowAsk's black, blue, coral, yellow, doodle, cat, font, line-weight,
or motion treatment to the portable skill. Do not add automatic aesthetic
acceptance, a style detector, an asset registry, or a mandatory
`cultivate → shape → design → review` pipeline.

## Post-rewrite verification record

All three probes used a disposable project containing only the revised packaged
skill. They were fresh ephemeral evaluator runs, not a matched pre-rewrite
baseline, so they support action and routing claims without proving that the
rewrite alone caused the behavior.

### Action claim

- **Probe and supplied artifacts:** review a described black-cat logo whose
  fine texture conflicts with heavy geometric landing illustrations, a focused
  survey, and a quiet dense dashboard; recommend the smallest revision and
  required acceptance evidence.
- **Expected disconfirming observation:** restyle the whole UI, add mascot
  decoration to the dashboard, prescribe one contour treatment to every
  surface, or accept the candidate without a real consumer matrix.
- **Observed action:** the evaluator named rendering language as the main
  mismatch, kept the black-cat identity and differentiated surface density,
  proposed one simplified mark candidate, rejected dashboard decoration, and
  required real placements, consequential sizes, backgrounds, recognizability,
  and a human acceptance question.
- **Context path:** loaded `SKILL.md`, `commands/review.md`, the packaged
  Sequence fallback, and `references/visual-asset-production.md` after
  classifying the logo as the production object.
- **Transcript anchor:** ephemeral Codex thread
  `019f719f-a53c-7ca1-b538-1b6a7876820d`.
- **Verdict:** supported for this prompt; causal improvement over the former
  package remains unproven without a matched baseline.

### Boundary claim

- **Probe and supplied artifacts:** decide whether visual design owns a
  TypeScript JSON-parser return-type error with no rendered or user-visible
  effect.
- **Expected disconfirming observation:** dispatch a visual command, inspect
  presentation references, or invent a visual consequence.
- **Observed action:** the evaluator read only `SKILL.md`, assigned the task to
  ordinary functional TypeScript development/debugging, and stopped.
- **Transcript anchor:** ephemeral Codex thread
  `019f71a1-609d-7312-9ba5-d2f08127012f`.
- **Verdict:** supported.

### Context claim

- **Probe and supplied artifacts:** review a mobile settings-page field group
  with weak spacing and hierarchy inside an accepted system; a decorative
  thumbnail was present but explicitly unchanged.
- **Expected disconfirming observation:** load production-asset guidance merely
  because an image exists, load sibling operations, or broaden into a style
  exercise.
- **Observed loaded paths:** `SKILL.md` and `commands/review.md`.
- **Observed omitted paths:** `references/visual-asset-production.md`, the
  presentation model, source index, concepts, and sibling commands.
- **Observed action:** the evaluator kept the thumbnail out of scope and,
  because the disposable fixture had no screenshot or page source, declined to
  invent spacing values.
- **Transcript anchor:** ephemeral Codex thread
  `019f71a0-ee7f-7410-8fe6-0b17735f8d1c`.
- **Verdict:** supported; the absence of a rendered artifact also exercised the
  skill's evidence boundary.
