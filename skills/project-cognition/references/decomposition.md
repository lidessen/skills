# Adaptive Cognition Decomposition

Use decomposition to fit a substantial cognition question into investigations
that one agent can understand and verify reliably. Do not begin with a standard
folder list or facet taxonomy.

## Select from the concrete field

Start with the named future decisions and a low-resolution project whole. Find
where one bounded question can own a coherent explanation and where crossing a
boundary would require independent source, authority, state, or causal context.

Possible strategies include:

- responsibility or ownership boundaries;
- end-to-end causal or data paths;
- state lifecycle and recovery boundaries;
- authority and commit boundaries;
- public contract and consumer boundaries;
- changed-relation clusters for a revision delta;
- uncertainty or adversarial verification of load-bearing claims; and
- hybrids formed around the project's principal decision risk.

These are candidates. Select, combine, or reject them from actual evidence.
Different tasks in the same project should produce different plans when their
decision fields differ.

## Packet gate

Before execution, every packet should state:

```text
Question and decision contribution:
Named source revision and scope:
Incoming and outgoing relations:
Expected local artifact:
Local acceptance and source checks:
What must be reported rather than silently expanding scope:
Overload or repartition signal:
```

Keep the whole visible at low resolution. Deliberate overlap is allowed where
two packets must independently check a load-bearing boundary, but record why it
exists.

## Adaptive recovery

A failed, overloaded, or invalidated packet does not require rerunning valid
siblings. Repartition only the affected semantic region, preserving its
incoming/outgoing obligations. A split is successful when the children make the
previously incoherent question locally verifiable and can reconnect without
inventing a new source relation.

## Synthesis gate

Synthesis is reconstruction, not concatenation or voting. It must:

1. reconcile packet source revisions;
2. reconnect every declared incoming/outgoing relation;
3. resolve incompatible claims against source evidence;
4. retain unknown or missing relations explicitly;
5. show which future decision each retained facet supports; and
6. keep raw reports outside default context unless a check needs them.
