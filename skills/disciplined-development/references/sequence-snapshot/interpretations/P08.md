# P08 — Interpretation

**Sequence source:** P08｜可证伪性｜科学哲学

> Read P08 in `principles/SEQUENCE.md` first.

## Shared reading

An empirical claim intended to guide reliable learning should expose what could
show it wrong. Name an observation, test, or counterexample that would force
revision; a conclusion protected from all possible failure cannot guide this
kind of inquiry.

## Decision questions

- What observation would count against this claim?
- Is the proposed check capable of changing the decision, or merely confirming it?

## Boundary and common misreading

Not every value or creative direction is falsifiable in this narrow sense. Do
not confuse a passing test with fact admission; P13 governs that transition.

## Theoretical roots

- Karl Popper, *The Logic of Scientific Discovery* and *Conjectures and
  Refutations* — falsifiability as a criterion for empirical claims.

## Expression probes

- `evidence-driven` records a falsifiable observation before claiming progress.
- A candidate record supplies a counterexample or boundary that could defeat it.

## Revision discipline

Revise from a failed expression probe, counterexample, or corrected source root.
Clarify what can disconfirm a claim. A new verifier or authority boundary
belongs to P11 or P13.
