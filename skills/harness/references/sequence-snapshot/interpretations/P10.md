# P10 — Interpretation

**Sequence source:** P10｜有限人类带宽｜人机协作

> Read P10 in `principles/SEQUENCE.md` first.

## Shared reading

Human attention, review capacity, and responsibility are scarce even when agent
throughput is abundant. Put human judgment on the small skeleton whose failure
invalidates the rest; let agents prepare or rework non-acceptance detail that
can be checked, discarded, or regenerated without reopening that skeleton.

## Decision questions

- Which decisions irreducibly require human judgment or consent?
- What detail can be made cheap to inspect, reject, or regenerate?

## Boundary and common misreading

Scarcity does not justify skipping needed review. It asks for a better allocation
of review, not a lower standard of responsibility.

## Theoretical roots

- Herbert A. Simon, *Administrative Behavior* and bounded rationality.
- Project evidence: design-driven’s 30/70 skeleton and human review boundary.

## Expression probes

- A review presents acceptance-relevant evidence before implementation detail.
- A review exposes the human acceptance skeleton and links regenerable detail
  instead of requiring a human to inspect every generated artifact.

## Revision discipline

Revise from a failed expression probe, counterexample, or corrected source root.
Clarify allocation of scarce review capacity. A rule about context layout belongs
to P09; a minimal action-choice rule belongs to P15.
