# P15 — Interpretation

**Sequence source:** P15｜只选择化解当前矛盾且保留硬约束的最小有效跃迁｜控制论 / 动态规划

> Read P15 in `principles/SEQUENCE.md` first.

## Shared reading

After identifying the present contradiction and hard constraints, choose the
smallest transition that actually changes the situation. “Smallest” measures
unnecessary scope, coordination, and irreversible surface—not a license to omit
verification, consent, or a required settlement.

## Decision questions

- Which option resolves the named contradiction while preserving every hard constraint?
- What cost or risk does each additional step remove, and is it needed now?

## Boundary and common misreading

Do not turn local minimalism into denial of a system-level change when that
change is the actual minimum valid transition.

## Theoretical roots

- Control theory and dynamic programming’s constrained action selection.
- Project evidence: agent-worker’s smallest valid transition and adaptive
  workflows rather than universal stages.

## Expression probes

- Invoke `attention-driven` only where attention allocation is the live problem.
- Split a task only when the split reduces a named conflict, context, or blast radius.
- Before adding or merging a control structure, map each relevant disturbance to
  its response, verification, and recovery path; retain or add a distinction
  only when an existing path cannot preserve that hard constraint.

## Revision discipline

Revise from a failed expression probe, counterexample, or corrected source root.
Clarify costs and hard constraints. A new diagnosis of which contradiction leads
belongs to P04.
