# P12 — Interpretation

**Sequence source:** P12｜外化记忆与代际继承｜分布式认知

> Read P12 in `principles/SEQUENCE.md` first.

## Shared reading

When a judgment must survive a session, person, or generation, externalize a
sufficient durable artifact that lets a later actor recover the relevant reason.
Memory is not a transcript dump: it is organized inheritance that can change a
future decision. Continuity belongs to what a successor can recover and use, not
to keeping the original session, person, skill, or project perpetually active.

## Decision questions

- What must a later actor know to avoid redoing or contradicting this judgment?
- What can be compressed, regenerated, or safely forgotten?
- If the current carrier retires, what must a successor recover, and what
  observation would show that the inheritance was sufficient?

## Boundary and common misreading

Retaining a record does not make it authoritative fact, and externalizing an
inheritance does not by itself authorize retirement or destructive replacement.
P13 and P14 still govern verification and the fact authority of sources versus
projections; P15 decides whether repair, regeneration, or retirement is the
minimum valid transition.

## Theoretical roots

- Edwin Hutchins, *Cognition in the Wild* — cognition distributed across people,
  artifacts, and organized activity.

## Expression probes

- A design document preserves the architectural skeleton beyond one agent run.
- A candidate/review record lets later cultivation examine why a P-ID was adopted.
- A regenerated skill preserves source links, decision gates, and behavior
  evidence without inheriting the old prompt shape by default.

## Revision discipline

Revise from a failed expression probe, counterexample, or corrected source root.
Clarify the sufficient inheritance artifact. A new source-of-truth rule belongs to
P14 rather than P12.
