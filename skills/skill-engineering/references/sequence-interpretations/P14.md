# P14 — Interpretation

**Sequence source:** P14｜可由源重建者只是投影，不得拥有事实权｜系统论 / 事件溯源

> Read P14 in `principles/SEQUENCE.md` first.

## Shared reading

When a representation of the same state can be rebuilt from a named source, it
is a projection. It may optimize access, scoring, or display, but it must not
silently acquire fact authority merely because it is convenient or visible.

## Decision questions

- Can this state be reconstructed from another record without losing its meaning?
- If not, has it been explicitly promoted to an independently governed source?

## Boundary and common misreading

Not every pair of records is source and projection. A transcript, audit ledger,
and work log can be independent records with different semantics.

## Theoretical roots

- Event sourcing: Martin Fowler’s account of rebuilding state from a sequence of
  recorded events.
- Project evidence: source/projection boundaries in agent-worker, Shilu, Sikong,
  and Chiling.

## Expression probes

- An index can be deleted and rebuilt from authoritative entries.
- `SEQUENCE.md` is the semantic root of its interpretations; this is an
  authority relation, not a claim that their contextual guidance is reconstructible.

## Revision discipline

Revise from a failed expression probe, counterexample, or corrected source root.
Clarify reconstruction and authority tests. A new fact-admission rule belongs to
P13.
