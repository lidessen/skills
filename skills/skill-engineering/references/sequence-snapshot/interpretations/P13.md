# P13 — Interpretation

**Sequence source:** P13｜主张不等于事实；事实须经可追溯的验证提交｜实践论 / 控制论

> Read P13 in `principles/SEQUENCE.md` first.

## Shared reading

A proposal, report, successful-looking run, or attractive mapping is a claim;
it becomes shared fact only through traceable evidence, designated verification,
and recorded submission. This makes knowledge reusable without confusing it
with agent self-report.

## Decision questions

- What evidence links this claim to the stated result?
- Who verifies it, and where is the acceptance or settlement recorded?

## Boundary and common misreading

Exploration and drafts need not be submitted as durable facts. The gate applies
when a result would otherwise gain shared authority or close a commitment.

## Theoretical roots

- Practice epistemology: claims are tested in practice rather than declared true.
- Project evidence: `agent-worker` claim–verify–settle and Moniro’s
  explore–verify–codify correction.

## Expression probes

- A sequence candidate remains pending until human adoption is recorded.
- A task cannot close from a worker report alone.

## Revision discipline

Revise from a failed expression probe, counterexample, or corrected source root.
Clarify evidence and submission. A new authority split belongs to P11; a new
source/projection distinction belongs to P14.
