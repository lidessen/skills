# Rewrite an Existing Skill

Regenerate an inherited skill through concrete, abstraction, and concrete; do
not modernize its wording in place.

1. Read the complete existing skill, including every command, reference, script,
   and artifact that its body dispatches to. Record observed triggers, actual
   decisions, durable artifacts, source evidence, and recurring failures.
2. Separate that concrete inventory from contingent form: its name, phase
   count, directory layout, historical cross-skill story, and inherited rules.
   Keep source links; do not call inconvenient evidence noise.
3. Form a new target expression team using references/expression-team.md. The
   former Primary and Supporting P-IDs are provenance, not a verdict. If the
   abstract function is already owned elsewhere, propose merge, retirement, or
   demotion instead of rewriting a duplicate.
4. Recompose the retained function as a new skill. Preserve only material that
   enables its distinct action; move volatile or conditional material behind a
   direct reference.
5. Use references/evaluation.md to compare the old and new forms on the same
   action and boundary probes. Report regression risk and migration work before
   retiring the inherited form. Recompose expression through
   references/expression-layers.md; do not polish mixed register in place.

Do not silently change the Sequence or turn a project-local mechanism into a
general rule while rewriting.
