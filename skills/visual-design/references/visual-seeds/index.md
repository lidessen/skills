# Visual Seed Index

Use this index when an agent or human needs to compare actual visual work before
choosing a direction. A visual seed is a linked, inspectable artifact plus a
small observation card. It is neither a template nor evidence that its
treatment fits another project.

## Retention standard

Retain a seed because it demonstrates a coherent relation across several
formal dimensions, directs attention deliberately, performs a real job well,
and contributes a contrast not already present in the field. Fame, novelty,
personal admiration, and a style label are insufficient by themselves.

## Retrieval

1. Prefer supplied references and the host project's accepted and rejected work.
2. From this fallback index, select two seeds whose relations expose a
   consequential choice for the present content, audience, and artifact family.
   In a first direction pass, stop at exactly two and make one a counterpoint
   when an attractive direction could be wrong here. Retrieve a third only in a
   later iteration after human feedback or a real fragment exposes a missing
   material contrast.
3. Read only the selected cards, then open their live primary artifacts. A card
   whose artifact cannot be inspected is a discovery pointer, not visual
   evidence.
4. Separate direct observation from the card's prior interpretation. Record the
   decision each seed changes and what must not be copied.
5. Before returning, remove seed-specific nouns, motifs, and metaphors from the
   candidates unless the host project's own evidence independently requires
   them. Transfer the relation, not the source's identity vocabulary.
6. Ask the human to select, reject, or combine relations. Do not ask them to
   approve a source's surface wholesale.

No third-party screenshots or assets are bundled. Links and observations do not
grant reuse rights or imply endorsement by the source. Reinspect live artifacts
when their current form matters.
