# Expressive Research

## Use for

Deciding what an expressive direction must help people feel, notice, or do;
comparing emotional intent without separating it from usability and
accessibility; and allowing expression to vary without becoming arbitrary.

## Decision lenses

- [Google's research behind Material 3 Expressive](https://design.google/library/expressive-material-design-google-research)
  relates emotional attributes to color, shape, size, motion, containment,
  attention, task performance, preference, and accessibility across many
  studies. It is useful when a desired feeling must become a testable relation
  rather than a mood-board adjective.
- [Making Material You](https://design.google/library/making-material-you)
  asks how a system can permit personal expression, interpretation, and new
  rhythms without giving up consistency and accessibility. It is useful when
  coherence is being mistaken for uniform treatment.

## Transfer boundary

Recover the practice of naming intended perception, comparing variants with
people, testing attention and use, and designing a range of expression. Do not
copy Material components, rounded shapes, palettes, scale, motion, or Google's
emotional attributes as a universal vocabulary. Product research evidence does
not establish another project's identity.
