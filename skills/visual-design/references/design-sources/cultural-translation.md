# Cultural Translation

## Use for

Forming a visual language from a community, place, or historical relation while
avoiding cliché; distinguishing research and participation from motif
collection; and deciding which cultural source can truthfully change form.

## Decision lenses

- [The Juggernaut identity and website](https://www.pentagram.com/work/the-juggernaut)
  frames its problem as contemporary South Asian expression without predictable
  cliché, then connects type and pattern decisions to specific sign-painting,
  script, and woodblock histories.
- [Springster](https://www.design.studio/work/springster/)
  began with research with girls and local experts across several countries,
  then used the community proposition to guide illustration and a shared
  welcoming gesture. It shows why a broad demographic label is not yet a
  visual source.

## Transfer boundary

Recover field research, source specificity, community participation,
contemporary translation, and the explicit cliché check. Do not borrow scripts,
patterns, gestures, emoji forms, palettes, or illustration treatments. A case
study is process evidence, not permission to represent another culture.
