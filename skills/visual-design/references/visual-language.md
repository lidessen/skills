# Provisional Visual Language

Use this reference when a user supplies an underdetermined style cue such as
“hand-drawn doodle,” “editorial,” “retro,” or “playful,” or when later agents
need a cross-surface visual guide before a design system exists. Do not load it
for a local adjustment inside an accepted direction.

A style cue is evidence of desired perception, not a source of form or a
ready-made treatment. The job is to recover the relation the person is reaching
for, expose meaningful alternatives inside that family, and select enough
formal tendencies to guide making without inventing a specification.

Recover the relation, not the familiar props of its metaphor. A notebook-like
relation does not by itself justify paper texture, ruled grids, sticky notes,
handwriting fonts, or pencil icons. A construction relation does not require
rulers, gears, or blueprint decoration; a zine relation does not require tape
and torn-paper effects. Those may become local experiments only when a source,
content role, or real fragment earns them. Carry the relation first through
hierarchy, rhythm, gesture, material allocation, and the contrast between
expressive and functional surfaces.

## Expand the cue from concrete sources

Start from the project, audience, content, artifact family, supplied references,
and accepted or rejected preference evidence. Ask what the cue is meant to make
present: personal authorship, warmth, immediacy, construction, dissent, play,
public accessibility, craft, historical continuity, or another project-specific
relation. Do not equate the first adjective with that relation.

Build a small source field when the supplied evidence cannot expose real
contrasts. A recommendation requires at least one inspected concrete artifact.
When none is supplied or inherited, read `visual-seeds/index.md`, select exactly
two contrasting cards for the first direction pass, and inspect their live
artifacts. Retrieve a third only in a later iteration when human feedback or a
real fragment reveals a missing material contrast. Use
`design-sources/index.md` and at most one expressive-process detail only when
the formation or testing of a relation remains decision-relevant. Select
sources by the decision they can change:

- a direct artifact that performs a comparable job;
- a materially or culturally adjacent source that reveals another formal
  possibility inside the cue;
- a source from another medium whose production logic could transfer; and
- an attractive counterexample that would express the cue in the wrong way for
  this project.

Read primary artifacts where possible. For each retained source, separate
directly observed form, inferred effect, historical or production context,
decision changed here, and what must not be copied. A style name, mood board,
artist name, or resemblance is not sufficient evidence by itself.

A bundled seed card preserves discovery and a prior reading; it is not a local
copy of the work and cannot substitute for seeing the artifact. If the live
artifact is unavailable or the runtime lacks visual inspection, label the card
as an unverified pointer, state that limitation in the returned source field,
and do not claim its visual properties as direct observation. It may still
suggest a tentative comparison to inspect later; it cannot settle the
recommendation by itself.

## Resolve the visual language

Compare only dimensions that can change the present design:

- **gesture and edge:** controlled, hesitant, loose, forceful, broken, repeated;
- **shape construction:** geometric, cut, outlined, massed, assembled, skeletal;
- **rhythm and density:** sparse annotation, repeated motif, crowded field,
  isolated mark, sustained texture;
- **material and process:** pen, brush, crayon, print, collage, screen, vector,
  or a digital treatment that truthfully retains a production relation;
- **typographic relation:** neutral carrier, hand-made accent, integrated image,
  expressive display, or intentionally separate functional text;
- **color relation:** semantic restraint, material limitation, flat fields,
  irregular registration, monochrome mass, or another source-grounded role;
- **imagery and mark relation:** illustration, diagram, annotation, icon, logo,
  scene, pattern, or absence;
- **motion and interaction posture:** settled, responsive, one-shot, assembled,
  elastic, abrupt, or deliberately still; and
- **imperfection and repetition:** which variations communicate authorship or
  process, and which would only damage recognition, reading, or trust.

These dimensions are lenses, not axes that must all receive values. Keep only
those that distinguish candidates or constrain downstream action.

Form two or three candidates that represent different perceptual relations,
not cosmetic variants of one answer. Name each candidate by the relation it
makes present—not by a medium, period, genre, trend, or physical-object
metaphor. Derive the candidates from the project relation and source field; do
not retrieve a built-in list of named styles or treat common genre labels as a
taxonomy. Without inspected sources, label them as hypotheses and keep their
formal consequences relative rather than concrete.

For each candidate state only what distinguishes the choice:

```text
Relation made perceptible:
Source decisions inherited and transformed:
Relation-led formal tendencies that carry it:
Suitable artifact and content conditions:
Where expression should be strong, restrained, or absent:
Logo, icon/mark, illustration, component, type, and motion implications:
Plausible wrong reading and negative boundary:
Cost, production, accessibility, and maintenance consequences:
```

Run a metaphor check on each candidate: if removing the named props makes the
candidate collapse, it is a costume rather than a visual language. Re-form it
from the project relation and formal tendencies before comparing it. Also scan
candidate names, invariants, and asset implications for distinctive nouns,
motifs, or metaphors carried by a seed. Unless independent project evidence
requires them, remove them and retain only the relation the source helped make
visible.

Recommend one candidate when the evidence supports it. Present the alternatives
and tradeoffs to the human rather than asking them to invent the choice space.
Human selection remains the direction authority.

Candidate implications describe relations, not ready-made assets. Do not offer
logo-motif examples, mascots, icon metaphors, or illustration subjects merely
from the style cue. State whether a future or inherited mark would need a
different silhouette, construction, contour, mass, texture, color role, or
motion, and leave its content unresolved until identity sources establish it.
When identity sources are absent, limit logo guidance to expression strength
and dimensions to investigate; do not propose shapes, sizes, or techniques.

## Allocate expression across the artifact family

Coherence does not require equal expressive intensity. Determine the functional
skeleton and then allocate the visual language according to the audience's
decision:

- orientation and identity surfaces may carry the strongest expression;
- sustained reading needs stable rhythm, legible type, and controlled imagery;
- dense tools, forms, navigation, and recovery states usually need precise,
  predictable behavior even when their surrounding system is expressive;
- explanation, onboarding, empty states, diagrams, or transitions may carry a
  stronger illustrative voice when they do not become the only source of
  meaning; and
- ordinary downstream surfaces must retain enough of the language to belong to
  the same project without imitating the most expressive composition.

Choose the website or document form from the content, intended action, and
visual relation. A campaign, publication, portfolio, community space, knowledge
base, and operational tool should not receive the same composition merely
because the same style cue was used.

For a logo or recurring mark, preserve the identity conditions that matter at
consequential sizes and backgrounds. Decide whether the language changes its
silhouette, construction, contour, mass, texture, color role, or motion—not
whether a stylistic filter can be applied. Read `visual-asset-production.md`
when the asset becomes a primary production object.

## Return a provisional guide

Produce only enough guidance for another agent to continue concrete design.
This artifact chooses a relation and allocates expression; it does not choose
or illustrate its treatment:

Unless an inherited source or inspected artifact already fixes them, do not
name exact font families, color values, pixel sizes, token names, grid values,
component treatments, or production technologies. Express formal relations,
selection criteria, and open variables. The first real fragments exist to test
those variables, not to fill them in by convention.

A guide remains provisional only when it can survive materially different
treatments. If its identity depends on a named prop, particular component,
specific drawing recipe, or fixed value, move that claim into a later fragment
test or remove it.

```text
Project, audience, and intended relation:
Style cue and ambiguities resolved:
Source field, observed decisions, and transfer limits:
Candidates considered and material tradeoffs:
Selected provisional visual language:
Formal tendencies that carry the relation:
Functional skeleton and attention constraints:
Expression allocation across representative surfaces:
Logo, icon/mark, illustration, component, typography, color, and motion implications:
Smallest shared invariants:
Dimensions deliberately left open:
Negative boundary and plausible wrong readings:
First representative fragments or surfaces to test:
Human selection evidence and unresolved question:
Observation that would revise or reject the guide:
Status, owner, and review date:
```

This guide is project-local and provisional. It is not a palette, component
inventory, prompt recipe, brand book, or design system. A downstream agent must
preserve its relation, invariants, open dimensions, and negative boundary while
adapting the treatment to the actual content and surface.

## Promote only through practice

Test the language on one expressive surface and one ordinary downstream
surface. Retain what survives different content, state, size, and production
conditions. Only repeated decisions with independent real consumers may become
tokens, component rules, illustration grammar, logo derivatives, or motion
language. Record rejected assumptions so later agents do not convert one
successful treatment into a false universal style.
