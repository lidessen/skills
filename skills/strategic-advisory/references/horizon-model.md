# Rolling Horizon Model

The three horizons differ by commitment, not merely by calendar distance. Choose
actual dates only when the human mandate supplies them.

| Horizon | It contains | It excludes | Revision trigger |
|---|---|---|---|
| Long direction | concrete future condition, protected boundaries, strategic thesis, and work-family scale where material | task lists, staffing, fixed implementation sequence, executable caps | principal contradiction or boundary evidence changes |
| Medium capability hypothesis | a small capability/program set, dependencies, evidence gaps, capability indicators, and scenario envelope where material | automatic resource commitment or detailed work queue | a capability test fails, dependency changes, forecast overlap blocks choice, or the long direction changes |
| Short mission candidate | one to three minimum practices, linked capability/direction, acceptance evidence, Work Estimate, and envelope after approval | unbounded decomposition, automatic child tasks, or self-approved spend | approval withheld, acceptance evidence fails, envelope boundary is reached, or a stronger contradiction appears |

## Link rule

```text
short mission candidate
        ↓ builds or tests
medium capability hypothesis
        ↓ makes possible
long direction
```

If a short mission has no link upward, it is ordinary work rather than strategy.
If a long direction has no plausible capability path, it is aspiration rather
than a strategic recommendation.

## Replanning

Replanning is triggered by a material failed assumption, changed boundary,
capability result, or new principal contradiction—not by the mere passage of a
calendar interval. A fixed five-year window may be the human mandate, but it
does not turn uncertain middle and long layers into committed task lists.
